# Belief System — Expression

**Version:** 0.1
**Status:** Emerging — not yet validated
**Tags:** #belief-system #expression #foundation

---

## Prime Directive

An expression is a derived artifact — a lossy projection of a lossless source, calibrated to a specific receiver and channel. The source holds everything. The expression holds what the receiver can use.

---

## Core Beliefs

### 1. The source is lossless; expressions are derived
The lossless source lives in Yherda. Expressions — skill files, human documents, agent contexts — are generated from it. Editing an expression directly is editing a derived artifact; the change will be lost at the next generation.

### 2. When the source changes, regenerate the expression
A retro updates the model. The expression then regenerates from the updated source. The expression tracks the source — it does not replace it.

### 3. Lossiness is calibrated to the receiver, not a failure of fidelity
Every expression drops something. What gets dropped is a design decision: what does this receiver need to reason or act well? A lossless expression delivered to the wrong channel is not high fidelity — it is noise at full resolution.

### 4. The lossy decisions require judgement, not rules
Deciding what to drop cannot be fully mechanized. It requires understanding the receiver, the channel, and what the content is worth. This judgement improves through retro — capture decisions and outcomes; the pattern becomes a belief.
